
### Common Medical Conditions and Symptoms

1. **Common Cold**
   - Symptoms: Runny nose, sore throat, cough, congestion, mild body aches, sneezing, low-grade fever.
   - Cause: Viral infection of the upper respiratory tract.
   - Treatment: Rest, fluids, over-the-counter decongestants or antihistamines.

2. **Influenza (Flu)**
   - Symptoms: High fever, chills, muscle aches, fatigue, sore throat, dry cough.
   - Cause: Influenza virus.
   - Treatment: Antiviral medication (e.g., oseltamivir), rest, hydration.

3. **Migraine**
   - Symptoms: Throbbing headache (often one-sided), nausea, sensitivity to light and sound.
   - Triggers: Stress, hormonal changes, certain foods, lack of sleep.
   - Treatment: Pain relievers, triptans, lifestyle changes.

4. **Strep Throat**
   - Symptoms: Sudden sore throat, pain when swallowing, fever, swollen lymph nodes.
   - Cause: Streptococcus pyogenes bacteria.
   - Diagnosis: Rapid strep test or throat culture.
   - Treatment: Antibiotics (e.g., penicillin, amoxicillin).

5. **Pneumonia**
   - Symptoms: Chest pain, cough with phlegm, fever, chills, difficulty breathing.
   - Cause: Bacterial, viral, or fungal infection in the lungs.
   - Diagnosis: Chest X-ray, blood tests.
   - Treatment: Antibiotics (if bacterial), rest, oxygen therapy if severe.

6. **COVID-19**
   - Symptoms: Fever, cough, shortness of breath, fatigue, loss of taste/smell.
   - Transmission: Respiratory droplets, airborne particles.
   - Prevention: Vaccination, masks, hand hygiene.
   - Treatment: Supportive care, antivirals (e.g., Paxlovid).

7. **Sinus Infection (Sinusitis)**
   - Symptoms: Facial pain, nasal congestion, headache, postnasal drip.
   - Cause: Viral or bacterial infection of the sinuses.
   - Treatment: Decongestants, saline sprays, antibiotics (if bacterial).

---

### Diagnostic Guidelines

- If a patient has a fever, sore throat, and cough, consider **flu, common cold, or COVID-19**.
- If there is a sudden severe headache with light sensitivity, consider **migraine**.
- If cough produces green/yellow mucus and there's chest pain, consider **pneumonia**.
- If the patient has swollen tonsils and pain swallowing, consider **strep throat**.

---

### Advice

- Always recommend consulting a licensed healthcare provider for formal diagnosis.
- Note patient history, allergy information, and symptom progression.
